PROGRAM

Here are some key programs involved in the implementation of R.A. 10630, the Juvenile Justice and Welfare Act of 2013 in the Philippines:

Bahay Pag-asa Centers:

Residential facilities for CICLs aged 15-18 who have committed serious offenses. Provide rehabilitation, education, skills training, counseling, and healthcare. Aim to address the underlying causes of their behavior and prepare them for reintegration into society.

Intensive Juvenile Intervention and Support Centers (IJISCs):

Temporary shelters for CICLs awaiting trial or undergoing intervention programs. Provide assessment, counseling, education, and other support services. Aim to divert CICLs from the formal justice system whenever possible.

Community-Based Intervention Programs:

Non-residential programs for CICLs who do not require placement in Bahay Pag-asa or IJISCs. Offer diversion, counseling, skills training, education, and family support services. Aim to address the needs of CICLs within their own communities and prevent further involvement in the justice system.

Family Reintegration Programs:

Support the reintegration of CICLs back into their families and communities after completion of intervention programs. Provide counseling, mediation, and other support services to help families address underlying issues and strengthen relationships.
